HuMoGen-X: User-Guided Editing, Genre-Aware 3D Motion Generation via Music-Conditioned Diffusion

Daye Lee1 Taesup Kim1,*
1Seoul National University *Corresponding author (Advisor)
Paper (soon) Code Live Demo Dataset (soon)

Abstract

We introduce HuMoGen-X, a controllable diffusion-based framework for 3D dance motion generation conditioned on three user-defined inputs: music, dance genre, and keyframes. While prior models primarily focus on music alignment or implicit genre control, HuMoGen-X enables explicit and disentangled conditioning across modalities, allowing users to guide choreography both stylistically and structurally.

To support this framework, we construct a novel dataset, CrossGenreSet, which comprises 12 distinct dance genres and includes both genre-aligned and genre-mismatched pairs. This dataset not only increases motion diversity but also enables robust evaluation under complex multi-conditional control scenarios. Our model extends the denoising diffusion probabilistic model by incorporating a Transformer-based decoder and introduces Chained Classifier-Free Guidance (Chained CFG) to generate realistic and high-quality 3D dance motions conditioned simultaneously on music, genre, and keyframes. Furthermore, we integrate an additional independent single-condition CFG mechanism, allowing fine-grained modulation of each input condition—music, genre, and keyframe—during the sampling process.

To quantitatively assess the genre consistency of the generated motions, we introduce a lightweight genre classifier tailored for the HuMoGen-X framework. Experimental results demonstrate that HuMoGen-X consistently outperforms existing baseline models across multiple metrics, including structural alignment ($MPJPE_{k}$, $MPJPE_{g}$), rhythm-to-music synchronization (Beat Alignment), and genre fidelity (F1 score, Top-1 accuracy).

These findings underscore the significance of cascaded genre integration - evidenced by t-SNE visualizations—and the effectiveness of multi-conditional control enabled by the proposed Chained Classifier-Free Guidance (Chained CFG) mechanism. Collectively, they establish HuMoGen-X as a practical and expressive framework for interactive choreography generation.
